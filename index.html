My name is Daniel Howard. Friends also call me DHow ("Dee-How").

I am a graduate student Ph.D. candidate at the University of Notre Dame in the Department of Applied & Computational Mathematics & Statistics. In 2015, I graduated from the University of Wisconsin-Madison with a B.S. degree in Applied Mathematics, Engineering, & Physics.

My current research work focuses on computational fluid dynamics and numerical modeling of Indian monsoons utilizing High Performance Computing machinery. This work is under my advisor Prof. Harindra Joe Fernando with the Environmental Fluid Dynamics Group.

Alongside my interests in CFD and atmospheric dynamics, I'm also passionate about sustainability, energy, and environmental & social justice through groups like Fossil Free ND. At Notre Dame, I'm also the president of the SIAM Student Chapter and ACMS Student Organization.
